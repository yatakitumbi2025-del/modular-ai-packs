# Percentages

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" means "per hundred", so 15% means 15 per 100, or the fraction 15/100, or the decimal 0.15.

## Finding a percentage of a number

To find X% of a number N, convert the percentage to a decimal and multiply: `(X / 100) * N`. For example, 15% of 240 is `0.15 * 240 = 36`.

## Percentage increase and decrease

To increase a number by X%, multiply it by `(1 + X/100)`. A 20% increase on 50 is `50 * 1.20`. To decrease by X%, multiply by `(1 - X/100)`. A 20% discount on 50 is `50 * 0.80`.

## Expressing one number as a percentage of another

To express A as a percentage of B, divide and multiply by 100: `(A / B) * 100`. If 30 of 120 seats are taken, that is `(30 / 120) * 100 = 25%`.

## Percentage change between two values

The percentage change from an old value to a new value is `((new - old) / old) * 100`. A positive result is an increase, a negative result is a decrease.
